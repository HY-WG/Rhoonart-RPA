# Staff Interview Guide: Building a Practical CLAUDE.md

Questions to ask working staff to extract implicit knowledge for configuring Claude effectively.

---

## Category 1: Their Work & Role
> *Understand who Claude is helping*

- What is your job title and what do you actually do day-to-day?
- What does a typical workday look like for you?
- What are the top 3 tasks that consume most of your time?
- What skills or domain knowledge does your role require?
- Who do you collaborate with most, and how?

---

## Category 2: What They Want Claude to Do
> *Define the actual job*

- What tasks do you wish you could hand off or get help with?
- What do you currently do manually that feels repetitive or tedious?
- What decisions do you make often that follow a pattern?
- Where do you spend time searching for information?
- What outputs do you produce? (reports, emails, code, documents, data)

---

## Category 3: Tools & Systems
> *Map the technical environment*

- What software/tools do you use every day? (Excel, SAP, Slack, Jira, etc.)
- What file formats do you work with most? (CSV, PDF, JSON, etc.)
- Where is your data stored? (shared drives, databases, cloud storage)
- Are there any systems Claude should never touch or modify?
- Do you have access to APIs or is everything UI-based?

---

## Category 4: Rules, Exceptions & Edge Cases
> *This is the most valuable category — institutional knowledge lives here*

- What are the unwritten rules in your process that aren't in any manual?
- When does a "normal" process break down? What are the exceptions?
- What mistakes have happened before that we must avoid repeating?
- What requires a human decision and should never be automated?
- Are there approval steps or sign-offs required before action is taken?
- What happens if something goes wrong mid-process?

---

## Category 5: Data & Confidentiality
> *Compliance and sensitivity*

- What data is sensitive or confidential in your work?
- Is there personal data (PII) involved? (names, IDs, financials)
- Are there regulatory requirements you must follow? (GDPR, SOX, HIPAA)
- What should Claude never log, store, or expose?
- Who is allowed to see the outputs Claude produces?

---

## Category 6: Quality & Standards
> *Define "done correctly"*

- What does a correct output look like? Can you show an example?
- What does a wrong output look like? What are common mistakes?
- How do you currently validate that work is correct?
- Are there templates, style guides, or formats you must follow?
- What tone or language is expected? (formal, technical, plain English)

---

## Category 7: Priorities & Constraints
> *How to handle tradeoffs*

- When speed and accuracy conflict, which wins?
- What is the worst thing that could happen if Claude makes an error here?
- Are there hard deadlines or time windows for certain tasks?
- What should Claude do if it is unsure — stop and ask, or make a best guess?

---

## Category 8: Feedback & Improvement
> *Ongoing calibration*

- How will you know if Claude's help is working or not?
- How do you want to give Claude feedback when it does something wrong?
- What would make you trust Claude enough to rely on it fully?
- What would make you stop using it?

---

## Interview Tips

| Tip | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Ask for **real examples**, not hypotheticals | Staff describe the ideal process, not the actual one |
| Ask "what went wrong last time?" | Surfaces edge cases and failure modes |
| Shadow the process if possible | Reveals steps they forgot to mention |
| Ask "who else should I talk to?" | Uncovers stakeholders with veto power |
| Record or take notes verbatim | Their exact words often become CLAUDE.md rules |

---

## Key Insight

The answers to **Category 4 (Rules & Exceptions)** are usually the most valuable.
That is where institutional knowledge lives that no documentation captures.
